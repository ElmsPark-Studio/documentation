Email Setup Guide

Set up reliable email with Mailgun

Stop your site's email landing in spam. The recommended way to send from any PageMotor site.

⏱ ~20 minutes 💷 Free to 100/day 🌍 Pick your region once
Why bother? Shared hosting sends email with a basic built-in method that big providers like Gmail and Outlook quietly distrust, so your messages land in spam or vanish. Mailgun is the recommended fix: it sends your mail properly signed and authenticated, so it actually arrives. The free plan covers 100 emails a day, which is plenty for most sites.
You will need: your domain name, a way into wherever your domain's DNS is managed (often Bluehost or Cloudflare), and your PageMotor admin login.

The six steps

  1. Create a Mailgun account
  2. Add your sending domain
  3. Add the DNS records
  4. Create a sending key
  5. Connect PageMotor
  6. Prove it works

Trouble?

  1. Jump to troubleshooting →

1Create a Mailgun account

  1. Sign up free at mailgun.com. No credit card is needed for the free plan.
  2. During signup you choose a Region. Pick EU if your visitors are mainly in the UK or Europe, or US otherwise.
Choose the region carefully. It is locked to your domain for life and cannot be changed later. EU is the right call for UK and Irish audiences.

2Add your sending domain

  1. In Mailgun, go to Send → Domains and click Add new domain.
  2. Enter mg.yourdomain.com (your real domain with mg. in front).
  3. Leave the suggested settings as they are and confirm.
Why the mg. bit matters: Mailgun sends from a dedicated sub-name, so your normal email (anything arriving at your main domain) is never touched. Keep the mg. prefix.

3Add the DNS records

This is the step that proves to the world that Mailgun is allowed to send for you. Mailgun shows you a short list of records: an SPF line, one or two DKIM records (Mailgun has two DKIM setups and both work, so seeing only one is normal), a tracking record, and two MX lines.

  1. The easy way: on the records screen, look for Mailgun's Automatic setup ("Log in to your provider"). It supports Bluehost, Cloudflare and others. Approve it and Mailgun adds every record for you.
  2. By hand: if your provider is not supported, copy each record into your DNS panel exactly as shown. Do not change or shorten the values.
  3. Back in Mailgun, click Check status until every record turns verified. Give the DKIM records a few minutes to switch fully on.
  4. When Mailgun offers DMARC, turn it on and add the one extra record it gives you.
Golden rule: only ever one SPF and one DMARC record per name. Because Mailgun uses the mg. sub-name, it never clashes with email you already receive on your main domain.

4Create a sending key

  1. In your Mailgun domain settings, open the Sending keys tab and click Add sending key.
  2. Give it a name you will recognise, create it, and copy the key straight away. Mailgun shows it only once.
Keep this key somewhere safe, like your password manager. It is what lets PageMotor send through your Mailgun account.

5Connect PageMotor

  1. In your PageMotor admin, go to Plugins → Manage Plugins → Upload.
  2. Install the EP Suite Mailgun plugin: ep-email-mailgun handles everyday email (contact forms, password resets). If you also send newsletters, add ep-newsletter-mailgun too.
  3. Open the plugin's settings and fill in: your Sending Key, the Sending Domain mg.yourdomain.com, and the Region that matches the one you chose in Mailgun.
  4. Click Test Connection. It should turn green.

6Prove it works

  1. Send yourself a real test, through a contact form or the plugin's test button. It should land in your Inbox, not spam.
  2. For a thorough check, send a message to the address shown at mail-tester.com, then read your score. Aim for 9 or 10 out of 10.
One honest warning: Mailgun saying "verified" only means it will accept your mail. It does not prove the message reached someone's inbox. Believe it is done only once a real test email actually arrives where you can see it.

If something is not working

Open these only if you hit them.

My test email still lands in spam

Usually the DKIM records have not finished switching on. Wait a few minutes and check Mailgun again. Make sure your From address uses your own domain, not a free address like gmail.com. A run through mail-tester.com will name whatever is still missing.

Mailgun says "verified" but mail will not send

After the records verify, Mailgun still needs a few minutes to switch the DKIM keys fully active. Refresh the domain page until the warnings clear, then try the test again.

It warns about a duplicate SPF or DMARC record

There can only be one SPF and one DMARC record per name. You should not hit this with the mg. setup, but if you do, edit the existing record rather than adding a second one.

I am on WordPress, not PageMotor

Same Mailgun account and DNS, different last step. Use Gravity SMTP if you have a Gravity Forms licence (note: it needs Mailgun's account-level Private API key, not the sending key above), or the free official Mailgun plugin otherwise.
